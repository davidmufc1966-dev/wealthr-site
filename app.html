❓ Frequently Asked Questions
Tap a question to expand the answer.
Is my data safe?
›
Yes — all your financial data is stored only on your device using localStorage and IndexedDB. Nothing is ever sent to our servers or shared with anyone. We do not collect, store or transmit your financial data.
Does the app work offline?
›
Most features work offline. Stock prices, crypto prices, market data and news need an internet connection to refresh, but all your saved data is always accessible without one.
What is Wealthr Pro?
›
Wealthr Pro is a £1.49/month subscription that unlocks unlimited accounts, net worth history, property and pension tracking, finance news, 30+ currencies, CSV import/export, bill reminders, recurring transactions and more. Managed via Google Play billing — cancel anytime.
How do I upgrade to Pro?
›
Tap the ✨ Upgrade banner on the Overview tab, or go to ⚙️ Settings and tap the Pro section. Payment is handled securely by Google Play.
How do I cancel my Pro subscription?
›
Open Google Play → tap your profile → Payments and subscriptions → Subscriptions → Wealthr → Cancel. You keep Pro until the end of your billing period.
Can I use this on multiple devices?
›
Use ⚙️ Settings → Export Backup to download your data as a JSON file, then Import Backup on your other device to restore everything.
How do I export my data?
›
⚙️ Settings → Your Data → 💾 Export Backup. A file named wealthr-backup-[date].json downloads instantly containing all your data. Pro users can also export portfolio holdings as CSV.
Where does the backup save to?
›
📱 iPhone — Files app → Downloads folder. You may see a share sheet asking where to save it. 🤖 Android — Downloads folder, you will get a notification when done.
How do I restore from a backup?
›
⚙️ Settings → Your Data → 📂 Import Backup. Select your .json file. The app restores everything and reloads automatically.
How often should I back up?
›
At least once a week, especially after adding accounts or transactions. If your browser data is cleared, your app data goes with it.
What is Total Net Worth?
›
Everything you own minus everything you owe — portfolio, bank, crypto, pension, property equity, minus credit cards and loans.
What is Liquid Net Worth?
›
Same as Total but excludes property equity and pensions since those cannot be accessed quickly. A more conservative measure of your actual spending power.
How does the history chart work?
›
Tap 📸 Save Today on the Overview tab to save a snapshot of your current net worth. Do this monthly to build a history chart over time. Up to 24 snapshots stored. This is a Pro feature.
How do live stock prices work?
›
Stock prices are fetched automatically via Twelve Data. US tickers work out of the box. Tap ↻ Refresh on the Portfolio or Overview tab to update prices.
How do I add UK stocks?
›
Add tickers with the .LON suffix — e.g. BARC.LON, VOD.LON, LLOY.LON — and they will fetch automatically alongside your US stocks.
Why are stock prices slightly off?
›
Prices may have a short delay and convert from USD to GBP using a live exchange rate, so small differences from other apps are normal.
How do crypto prices work?
›
Via CoinGecko — free, no API key needed. Prices are in GBP and update when you tap ↻ Refresh on the Crypto tab or Overview.
My coin is not showing a price
›
Check you are using the correct symbol (BTC, ETH, SOL etc). Less common coins may not be supported. Verify the symbol on CoinGecko.
How do bill reminders work?
›
Add a bill in the Bills tab (Pro) with a name, amount, cycle and due date. The app checks for upcoming bills on open and sends a notification if one is due soon. Works best when installed as a PWA or via Google Play.
What billing cycles are supported?
›
Monthly (same date), every 4 weeks (28 days rolling), weekly, quarterly and annually. The app automatically tracks the next due date for each cycle.
How do I change my currency?
›
⚙️ Settings → Base Currency. Tap your preferred currency. All values convert automatically using live exchange rates. This is a Pro feature — free users stay in GBP.
What is FSCS?
›
The Financial Services Compensation Scheme protects UK bank deposits up to £85,000 per person per bank if a bank goes bust. Joint accounts get £170,000 protection.
Which banks are FSCS protected?
›
Most UK banks — Barclays, HSBC, Lloyds, NatWest, Santander, Halifax, Nationwide, Monzo, Starling and more. Always check with your provider to confirm.
What is NOT covered by FSCS?
›
Investments, stocks and crypto are not covered by deposit protection. Some platforms have separate FSCS investment protection but only for bad advice or fraud — not market losses.
What third party services does Wealthr use?
›
Twelve Data (stock prices), CoinGecko (crypto prices), open.er-api.com (exchange rates), Yahoo Finance / Guardian / BBC / Reuters / FT / Sky / CNBC (news feeds), Bank of England (base rate), Land Registry (sold prices), and corsproxy.io / allorigins.win (CORS proxies). These are only contacted when you actively use those features.
